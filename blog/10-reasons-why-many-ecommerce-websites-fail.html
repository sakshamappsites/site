10 Reasons why an Ecommerce website fails?

An Ecommerce website offers much more revenue. It gives the ability to be open 24/7 with traditionally fewer overheads when compared to bricks and mortar retailing. Yet, despite the fact that most of the consumers prefer to shop online, a huge number of ecommerce websites fail.

If you own an ecommerce store, or are planning on setting one, you are at the right place. Here we are 10 crucial items to be aware of that will have a significant impact on the success or failure of your ecommerce website.

Poor Quality Images and Product Description

When it comes to shopping online, customers are unable to pick up a product and take a good look at it. So instead, they rely on the images your ecommerce store provides. Following the descriptions provided for the product. If you are showcasing your product with poor quality photos or offer limited descriptions, you’ll almost definitely lose sales and ultimately a customer.

Always offer photos that display the product from multiple views. Use only high quality images, include zoom in option, and be sure to include unique detailed descriptions. Avoid using generic product descriptions which you already have from the supplier. Don’t rely solely on price. Tempt visitors with great imagery and product detail.

Missing Contact Information

It seems like a small issue, doesn’t it? But if not displaying full contact information, or its lost somewhere deep in the site, it results in major trust barrier for potential customers. Before customer buy, they will look for this information. Because they want peace of mind and convince themselves that if they need to contact you, they can. If you refuse to show location and contact information,  including a phone number. You are going to have a hard time establishing trust with potential customers.

Complicated Checkout

Ecommerce websites like Amazon, Flipkart have pioneered the simple purchase process. And keeping the payment process simple/easy they revolutionized ecommerce. Consumers always prefer simpler checkout methods with fewer steps. A complex, multi-step checkout process is much more time taking and results in frustrating experience for customers, and they’ll likely give up before completion of purchase.

Avoid Ecommerce website frustration by offering a very short checkout process. Also, don’t require registration for check out or viewing your product. Let customers go through the process and choose the option to register and save their details at the end, in case they want to come back. Otherwise let them checkout as a guest, without the need to register as many potential customers prefer to keep their personal information safe and avoid to register for the same reason.

Hidden Fees of Your

This is a big one. Cart abandonment is high as a result of this issue. Shipping rates should be shared upfront, at product selection. As customer selects the item keep showing them if they will be charged shipping, or on purchase of some more amount they will get free shipping. Hiding them until the very end is guaranteed to anger the buyer and ultimately lose them and never turn back. Provide your customers with a shipping calculator that works based on the items they’re interested in and their postal code. This is technically easy to achieve, and definitely you should offer this.

Big Screen, Small Screen

We’re a society moving more and more to mobile. And this is especially true for ecommerce. Get your ecommerce website responsive if it is not yet. If your online store hasn’t been properly designed for mobile, you will be offering clutter, poor navigation options and an incredibly difficult user experience for mobile users. Make sure your site is mobile responsive as most of the customers today use mobile to access internet and make purchases online.

Customers don’t know what to do

Even with an ecommerce website, you need to make it very clear what you want the customer to do on your site. Each and every thing should be cleared precisely to avoid confusions and best help your customer who is on your website to make the purchase. Non-product pages should direct the customer using a blatant and obvious call to action. The most successful ecommerce sites tell customers exactly what to do. Never leave customers to guess what the next step is be very clear with every next step. It is about guiding the user through your site, step by step, and ensuring they perform the actions you want them to as they might be not familiar with many things as you are, afterall its your store.

Online Marketing

Online Marketing comes with lot of challenges. In today’s internet world having an ecommerce store and selling online is dream of many people and why not? But having an store with all above points covered may also not be sufficient if end users don’t know about you. You might have an industry leading ecommerce store with an outstanding customer service However, without targeted traffic, you’ll not ever reach the heights you desire, or possibly not make enough sales to sustain the business.

Create an inbound marketing strategy, get in implemented and that will deliver leads from social media, Google organic and paid results and referral sources. Once you build up good sales, and n number of testimonials and reviews begin flowing in, that will have another positive impact for you.

You’re not Engaging Your Customers

Engage prospective and existing customers when and however possible. You can use social media to join the discussion, and keep your products and services in front of potential customers or those returning. If customer is not satisfied with any product and has returned it, connect with them. Talk to them, apologize if required. Try to understand what went wrong in the whole process and make sure its not repeated with another customers.

You’re targeting the Wrong Audience

Targeting the right audience is like achieving a milestone for an ecommerce store. You may have organic and paid search campaigns, along with comprehensive social media management. However, all this is wasted if the audience targeted is not right. Are you targeting generic terms that may not be appealing to people looking to purchase? Lot of research is needed here to select the right approach to generate sales. In addition, constant ongoing review and tweaking is required to make sure you are making the most of your budget.

Your Pricing is all Wrong

Pricing is a huge factor online and if not set properly, many ecommerce websites fail. Why to purchase from you when the customer is getting same thing, same brand from another store for lesser price. People can compare your pricing in seconds, instead of having to trudge around multiple physical stores. If your price is too low, customers might think your products are low quality. If price are too high, they’ll feel like you’re trying to rip them off and they will move on to another store.

Shipping is a factor here as well. Try to keep that as low as possible. Offer free shipping when possible, or on orders over a certain amount. Obviously this will be specific to what you are selling. Research and trial and error will definitely help you find the pricing sweet spot you need to gain and keep customers.

Summing Up

Ecommerce is a great way of starting a business with minimal overheads. However don’t make the mistake of believing it’s as simple as putting up an underwhelming site as fast as possible and putting products up without consideration. It needs a lot to turn a ecommerce website to a successful business. By keeping in mind the rules above and implementing them, you will hopefully succeed in your ecommerce ventures, and avoid the pitfalls that so many sites make.

The longer you wait to take action, the more you fall behind.
